# TraderBehavior_Vs_MarketSentiment


## Dataset Overview

* Analyzed 211,224 cryptocurrency trades from 32 traders.
* Combined trading data with Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index data from 2023 to 2025.
* The dataset was clean and also had almost no missing values.

## Key Findings

1. More Trading Happens During Fear

* Traders were most active when the market was in a Fear state.
* The highest trading volume was also recorded during Fear periods.

2. Extreme Greed Gives Better Results

* Traders earned the highest average profit during Extreme Greed periods.
* Winning trades were also more common during Extreme Greed.

3. Traders Buy More During Fear

* More buying activity was seen during Extreme Fear.
* More selling activity was seen during Extreme Greed.
* This suggests traders often buy when prices are low and sell when prices are high.

4. Sentiment Does Not Strongly Affect Trade Size

* Changes in market sentiment had very little impact on how large traders' positions were.
* Traders did not significantly increase or decrease trade sizes based on sentiment alone.

5. Top Traders Perform Better in Every Market Condition

* The best traders consistently earned much higher profits than lower-performing traders.
* Their performance remained strong during Fear, Neutral, and Greed market conditions as well.

6. Market Sentiment Alone Does Not Decide Profitability

* Fear and Greed periods showed similar profit levels overall.
* Other factors such as trading strategy and decision-making likely play a bigger role in success.

# Conclusion

Market sentiment affects how traders behave, but it is not the main reason behind profitability.

Successful traders were able to perform well in all types of market conditions, showing that trading skill and strategy are more important than market sentiment alone.
